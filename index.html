Biography

I am a pre-doctoral fellow in Economics at National University of Singapore. My interests lie at the intersection of Environmental and Development Economics. My research focus is on understanding the impacts of climate change in developing countries and designing interventions to encourage adaptation. Currently, I am conducting research on understanding the effects of floods, challenges in adaptation, and the role of social protection in Pakistan. I am excited about combining remote sensing data and spatial analysis with traditional survey methods in my research.

Working Papers

Unintended Consequences of Government Support: Impact of Pakistan’s Flood Relief Program on Adaptation Behavior

Combining household survey with spatial data on rainfall, nightlights, and population density, I investigate the effect of flood relief on adaptation behavior. I find that geographically targeted flood relief discourages community adaptation. I construct an economic model, consistent with the data, to explain the mechanisms.
Muhammad Bin Khalid

The Blessings of Scarcity: The Institutional Underpinnings of Small States’ Prosperity

We investigate the effect of state size at the time of independence on long run development. We use a mixed methods approach, combining an instrumental variable analysis with a comparative case study.

Work in Progress

Can aid prevent flood induced migration? Evidence from Pakistan’s 2010 floods (with Martin Mattsson)

▶ Details

Leveraging satellite data on settlement patterns, we construct a panel dataset of village populations spanning 1975 to 2020. Using a difference in differences approach, we study the effect of disaster relief in the wake of 2010 floods on migration behavior.

The effect of timely cash transfers during an extreme flood shock: Evidence from the 2022 Pakistan Floods (with Rocco Zizzamia & Ashley Pople)

▶ Details

Leveraging a discontinuity in the design of Pak’s 2022 flood relief program, we investigate the effect of cash transfers on household welfare. We conduct a 6000 household survey in rural Sindh and combine survey data with national socio-economic registry and spatial data on floods and rainfall.
